Rosé

Following harvest, the grapes are immediately pressed to deliver the most vibrant aromas. The organic grape juice is fermented and aged in stainless steel tanks with its skins until preparation for shipment.

Winemaker & Place

Eric Lanxade’s near four decade career crafting harmonious wines has been guided by a deep respect for Mother Nature. Graduating in Oenology from the University of Bordeaux in 1987, Eric has made wine in some of the most respected wine regions across the globe such as Bordeaux, Gascony, New Zealand, and Languedoc. Eric's winemaking taught him humility and fostered a commitment to environmental responsibility, developing a philosophy centered on the harmony of beautiful vineyards, believing that exceptional grapes and generous wines begin with nature's richness.

Situated between the steep slopes of the Pyrenees and the the Mediterranean Sea, with rolling hills and the garrigue flora, the vines of Pays D’Oc benefit from great natural wealth and an incredible diversity of soils, climates, and exposures to craft a perfect rosé.

Pairings

Democratizing fine wine means wine for all and wine without judgement! Wine and a box of mac and cheese? That sounds like a Tuesday night to us. However, if you did want to explore rosé wine pairings, Archer recommends sticking with more delicate foods that will benefit from the fruity notes and medium-body of our rosé. Perfect hot weather drinking.

* Fresh salads
* Charcuterie
* Seafood
* Soft cheeses
* Light pasta and rice dishes
* Raw or lightly cooked shellfish

Recipes

While rosé complements many dishes, seafood-centric dishes hit closest to home. Archer Roose Rosé does, after all, hail from the south of France adored for coastal port cities that boast breezy, sea-side cafés. Chef Adam Moore created a light, versatile Grilled Tuna Niçoise Salad that plays on all the wonderful seafood flavors we love, while keeping it simple.
